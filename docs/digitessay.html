Digit 100 Corpus Analysis Assignment

For the Corpus Analysis assignment, I was trying to think of a few possible

comparisons between the two works Hamlet and Macbeth. I decided to work with these

two because I have had to read each of them in the past, and because I thought it

would be pretty interesting to compare two of Shakespeare's work and see if there were

any similarities or differences in his work.



I tried to analyze a few different things for each work while trying to maintain a

sort of overarching theme. My first main theme was trying to find words that appear in

each work but have different meanings. As I was searching for words that matched this

idea, I found that the word 'water' appeared pretty frequently in each work, but had two

surprisingly different meanings. Once I found that this word was pretty common, I

increased the ngram size and tried to look at the context that this word was in and what

'water' meant in each context.



In Macbeth, water is extremely commonly used as a 'purifying word'. It appeared

alongside other words such as 'clears' and 'washes'. I was surprised that it was so

commonly referred to in this context, and that this context was a lot more common than

any other references. After comparing it to Hamlet's use of the word, I found it even

more interesting that the same word was used in such different ways even though each

piece was so similar (a tragedy) and was written by the same author.



In Hamlet however, the word was used almost specifically in the context of

'drowning'. It appeared commonly before the literal word 'drown' or 'drowns'. There were

only a few sentences where the word was not used under this context.



The next large theme that I decided to look at was the occurrence of death

related words compared to the amount of character deaths in each play. This topic

actually came out pretty interesting to me personally, because I found a pretty large

correlation between each of these.

I found that 'death' related words such as: death, dead, blood, died, appeared

pretty commonly in each as they are both tragedies. These words in particular appeared

93 times in Hamlet, while appearing 66 times in Macbeth. The interesting part to me is

that this means that these words appeared almost 1.5 times more likely in Hamlet

(1.41), and when looking at the amount of character deaths in each play there were 9 to

Hamlet and 5 to Macbeth. This means that there were 1.8 times more character deaths

in Hamlet than in Macbeth, which is relatively close to the amount of times the ratio of

the words per play.

I find this interesting because it makes me think that possibly Shakespeare had

written Hamlet with the preconceived idea of having more death and tragedy, and that it

might show in the correlation to the amount of times death and tragic related words

were used in each play, as well as the amount of character deaths per play. While I'm

not sure what to do with this information, I do find it pretty fascinating that there was

such a difference per each play, as I expected them to be somewhat similar; or at least

more similar than what the results had shown.
